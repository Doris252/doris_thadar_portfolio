About the event

NiCE Hack 2025 is a hardware cybersecurity hackathon co-organised by NTU's School of Electrical & Electronic Engineering and the National Integrated Centre for Evaluation (NiCE). The challenge focused on Side Channel Attacks (SCA) — a real-world hardware security technique used to extract secret cryptographic keys by analysing physical signals like electromagnetic emissions and power consumption, rather than attacking the algorithm itself.

What we did

Our team performed Side Channel Attacks on AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) hardware implementations. We used Electromagnetic (EM) analysis to capture signal traces from the target device, then applied Correlation Power Analysis (CPA) to statistically correlate those traces with hypothetical power models — ultimately recovering the secret AES key without ever breaking the encryption mathematically.
